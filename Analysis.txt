B. Consistency Analysis

In the consistency experiment, no misses were observed in 100 iterations. I also swapped the roles of the instances (writing to B and immediately reading from A), and still observed 0 misses.

This suggests that Firestore replication between regions is very fast under the current workload. The replication delay appears to be smaller than the network round-trip time in this experiment, so the newly written data was already visible when the second region was queried.

But we know Firestore does not imply strong consistency. Firestore guarantees eventual consistency, not strict read-after-write consistency across regions. The absence of misses in this experiment only indicates that replication is fast for this scale of workload.
